Onion Pakoda

In the vast Indian Cuisine there are many ways onion pakoda is made.

Ingredients

Onion 4 (finely sliced)
Gram Flour/Besan 1/2 cup
Rice Flour or Cornstarch 1 tsp
Grated Ginger 1 tsp
Fine Chopped Green Chilli 2
Turmeric 1/4 tsp
Red Chilli Powder 1 tsp
Carom Seed 1 tsp
Coriander Powder 1 tsp (optional)
Garam Masala 1 tsp (optional)
Fine Chopped Coriander Leaves
Salt to taste
Oil for Deep Fry

Instructions

1. To make Onion Pakoda, Take sliced onion in a large bowl.

2. Now add 1 tsp of salt, 1 tsp of grated ginger, and mix well.

3. Add chopped green chilli, 1/4 tsp of turmeric powder, red chilli powder, Cardamom seeds, garam masala, coriander powder chopped coriander leaves, 1/2 cup of gram flour, 1 tsp of rice flour and mix everything well using spoon or with your hands and squeeze the onions a couple of times so they begin to release moister.

4. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium flame. Check whether oil is sufficiently hot or not by dropping a pinch of mixture in hot oil if it comes upward immediately, then oil is sufficiently hot for deep frying. Take small portion of mixture in the hand and gently drop 3-4 small fritters into the oil and deep-fry until they turn golden brown in color keep stirring occasionally in between so that they cook evenly and keep heat/flame to medium.

5. Take them out using slotted spoon and drain excess oil. Place them on paper towel in a plate to absorb excess oil. Repeat the process for remaining batter and deep-fry in batches. Do not over crowd the oil with onion fritters, this will help them cook evenly.

6. Now your perfect and tasty Onion Pakora is completely ready, you can serve Onion Pakoda with some fried salted green chillies and a green chutney or sweet chutney. This whole combo can also be served with the Indian chai.
